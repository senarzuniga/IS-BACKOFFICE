# 01 - Market Analysis (Paper, Corrugated, and Packaging Machinery)

## Executive Take
The global opportunity for used machinery trading in corrugated and packaging is driven by five factors:
1. Capex pressure: many converters need capacity but avoid high-cost new equipment.
2. Faster commissioning: used equipment can be acquired and relocated quicker than new-build lead times.
3. Footprint optimization: plant closures, M&A, and relocations create a continuous secondary-equipment supply.
4. Sustainability/regulation pressure: demand for productivity and lower waste pushes retrofits and automation.
5. Regional expansion: emerging markets seek proven equipment with lower entry cost.

For Ingecart, this creates a practical wedge: sell machine + turnkey relocation + startup support.

## Macro Demand Signals To Track Weekly
- New corrugated converting plants announced
- Mill/box-plant shutdowns and line replacements
- M&A transactions creating asset disposals
- Pulp/containerboard price volatility (buyers delay new capex, seek used)
- E-commerce and food-packaging demand shifts by region

## Priority Demand Regions (Pragmatic order)
1. Iberia and Southern Europe (proximity, execution speed, language advantage)
2. North Africa and Mediterranean corridor (cost-sensitive buyers, logistics feasibility)
3. LATAM (mid-cap converters, growing demand for value equipment)
4. Eastern Europe and Balkans (retrofit and replacement demand)
5. Select Asia buyers through partners (price-sensitive and transaction-driven)

## Where Ingecart Can Win
- Mid-market buyers needing throughput improvement without full greenfield capex
- Plants requiring relocation/installation support (not only machine purchase)
- Buyers with urgent timelines (quarterly delivery pressure)
- Companies needing financing or staged-payment flexibility

## Typical Buyer Pain Points
- "We cannot wait 9-18 months for a new machine"
- "Our budget is below new-OEM quotes"
- "We need a complete relocation partner"
- "We need assurance on machine condition and commissioning risk"

## Competitive Dynamics (Used Machine Segment)
- Marketplaces compete on visibility and volume.
- Brokers compete on speed and transaction intermediation.
- Integrators/service players win high-value deals by reducing operational risk.

Ingecart should position as: trusted technical-commercial operator, not listing-only broker.

## Recommended Strategic Positioning
"Used machinery with lower risk and faster production start"

Offer stack:
- Technical due diligence report
- Video evidence and performance baseline
- Dismantling + shipping + installation project management
- Commissioning protocol + operator training
- Optional maintenance and spare strategy (6-12 months)

## Risk Map And Mitigations
- Unknown machine condition -> mandatory pre-sale audit + acceptance protocol
- Buyer financing limitations -> staged payment and partner financing options
- Logistics/customs delays -> pre-approved logistics route templates per region
- Mismatch between expected and real output -> transparent benchmark tests
- Long sales cycle -> strict CRM stage management and deal qualification

## Practical Market Thesis For 2026-2027
Used-machine demand should remain structurally active where financing costs and uncertainty stay elevated. Companies will prioritize ROI, lead-time, and operational continuity. In this context, Ingecart's combined offer (machine + relocation + service) is commercially stronger than pure machine brokerage.
