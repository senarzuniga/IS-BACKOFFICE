# 05 - Demand Discovery and Qualification System

## Objective
Identify real demand early, avoid low-probability deals, and focus resources on opportunities with technical and commercial feasibility.

## Discovery Questions (must ask)

### Business urgency
- What capacity or quality issue are you solving right now?
- Why now, and what happens if you delay 6 months?

### Technical context
- What machine/process are you replacing or complementing?
- Which specs are non-negotiable (speed, format, board type, print quality)?

### Project constraints
- Target commissioning date?
- Site footprint and utility constraints?
- Planned shutdown windows?

### Economic criteria
- Budget range approved?
- Preferred commercial structure (capex, phased payment, leasing)?

### Decision process
- Who are the decision-makers and technical validators?
- What are the procurement and approval steps?

## Qualification Model (BANT+T)
- Budget: real and accessible
- Authority: decision path clear
- Need: pain is active and quantified
- Timing: deployment horizon defined
- Technical fit: equipment + site + output compatibility

Only move to proposal stage if at least 4/5 are validated.

## Red Flags (disqualify or park)
- No urgency and no timeline
- No decision-maker access
- Major technical mismatch with machine inventory
- Buyer asks only for price with no operational context
- Logistics/customs infeasible in planned timeline

## Opportunity Stages
1. Identified account
2. Contacted
3. Discovery completed
4. Technically pre-qualified
5. Commercial proposal sent
6. Negotiation
7. Won/Lost

## Minimum Data Before Proposal
- Discovery questionnaire completed
- Preliminary technical fit check
- Commercial scope selected (EXW vs turnkey)
- Clear next meeting with decision stakeholders

## Weekly Pipeline Governance
- Review all open opportunities every Friday
- Downgrade stale opportunities with no progress in 14 days
- Concentrate effort on top 10 opportunities by score
