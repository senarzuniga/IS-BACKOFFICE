Migration from Fixed Transfer Car (BHS) to an AMR Fleet

Key Integration Points and High-Density WIP Layout Design for a Corrugated Packaging Plant

A pragmatic engineering approach to replace centralized transfer-car topology with an AMR-driven distributed material handling strategy, improving floor utilization, resiliency and operational flexibility.

Prepared for: Cascades — Engineering & Operations

Executive Summary

Proposal: Replace the fixed transfer car (BHS) with an AMR fleet and multiple trident stations coupled to a WCS/AMR Fleet Manager. The goal is to increase usable floor density, remove single points of failure, and enable JIT line feeding with lower mechanical maintenance exposure.

This document presents the system comparison, core value drivers, a layout blueprint, flow dynamics, engineering considerations and recommended next steps for concept validation.

Key Value Drivers

Maximized Floor Space / High-Density WIP

Convert aisle and conveyor volume into compact blind-block storage served by AMRs.

Cellised storage with zero-clearance stacking increases available WIP capacity and reduces retrieval times when combined with optimized AMR routing heuristics.

Elimination of Single Point of Failure

Distributed delivery and multiple trident buffers replace centralized transfer dependencies.

AMR redundancy and fleet-level rerouting maintain throughput during equipment faults or maintenance windows.

Decoupled Operational Flows

Asynchronous buffering and prioritized JIT deliveries reduce line starvation.

Trident handoffs provide job-change isolation and allow lines to continue during upstream variability.

Reduced Mechanical Maintenance

Shift to serviceable robotic units and simple station hardware.

Predictable maintenance schedules for AMRs lower unplanned interventions and spare-part complexity.

Improved Board Protection / Waste Reduction

Controlled handoffs and soft handling reduce board damage.

Precise posing at tridents and lower transfer shock improves first-pass yield and reduces rework.

Material Flow Dynamics

  1. Inbound induction from corrugator: Boards are inducted into the trident station; AMRs remove stacks to deep-lane cells to avoid obstructing corrugator flow. WCS assigns storage addresses and monitors residency time.
  2. Deep-lane WIP allocation: Blind-block cells hold batches; AMRs perform consolidation and pre-staging to local tridents based on line demand forecasts.
  3. JIT converting line feeding: AMRs deliver sequenced boards to trident stations at the line, handling off with minimal mechanical transfer and correct orientation for immediate processing.
  4. Reverse logistics / overruns handling: Exceptions are isolated to quarantine cells and rework areas without blocking main flow; AMRs and WCS handle rerouting and exception lifecycles.

Engineering & Integration Considerations

Critical: define the WCS ↔ AMR Fleet Manager API and a clear I/O handshake for each trident station early in the project to reduce later integration risk.
  • WCS / Fleet coordination: Job reservation, preemption rules, position reporting, and heartbeat events to ensure robust fleet operation.
  • Trident integration: Mechanical locators, presence sensors, electrical interlocks and PLC I/O for job-complete acknowledgement.
  • Traffic orchestration: Dual-lane policies, negotiated passes, and deadlock avoidance strategies with fallback to local buffers.
  • Exception handling: Design degraded modes that allow manual override and safe-park procedures for blocked robots or stations.

Recommended Next Step: Concept Validation & Layout Engineering

We recommend a focused concept validation phase to de-risk the migration and deliver a validated layout and sizing package suitable for a pilot deployment.

  • Site survey and dimensional validation
  • Throughput simulation and bottleneck analysis
  • WIP capacity model and blind-block sizing
  • AMR fleet sizing and route modelling
  • Trident station interface definition and mock-ups
  • Phased migration plan and pilot deployment
  • ROI / OPEX vs CAPEX model
