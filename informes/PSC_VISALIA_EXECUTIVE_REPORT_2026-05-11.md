# PSC VISALIA Executive Report

## Audience
PSC Capex Committee, Plant Management, Operations Leadership, and project stakeholders.

## Executive Thesis
This is not a second-hand machine purchase. It is a controlled relocation and modernization program that turns a proven BHS corrugator base into a safer, more scalable, and more predictable plant platform for Visalia.

The project combines four value layers:
- recover and redeploy an industrial asset with known technical history,
- rebuild the line around current control and handling standards,
- reduce manual traffic and operational risk in the corrugator area,
- create a plant flow that is ready for future automation and serviceability.

## Decision in One Line
Proceed, but only after one formal gate is closed: align the final capex basis with the commercial sheet, confirm the site survey, and secure written BHS support for relocation and post-installation service.

## What PSC Is Buying
PSC is buying an operational reset around a known corrugator platform. The value is not only in the machine itself, but in the complete system around it:

- a rebuilt corrugated line,
- a cleaner and safer handling model,
- a more stable flow between corrugator, WIP, and conversion,
- a serviceable architecture for maintenance and future upgrades,
- a commercial framework that can be managed as a project, not as isolated equipment purchases.

## Scope of the Project
The internal project pack consistently describes six blocks of scope:

1. Core line: BHS corrugator base, 2500 mm class, duplex configuration.
2. Services: disassembly, marking, documentation, cranes, transport support, assembly, and commissioning.
3. Peripherals: glue kitchen, boiler, steam tubes, and related utilities.
4. Intralogistics improvement: conveyors, WIP handling, and output flow redesign.
5. Ingetrans: automated reel handling and internal transport system.
6. Civil works and permits: site preparation required for installation and utilities.

## Why This Investment Makes Sense
### 1. Technical de-risking
The PDF and working documents show a legacy base with upgrades over time, but also a natural risk of support dispersion, spare-parts complexity, and learning-curve issues if the line is not re-architected. The project addresses that risk directly through modernization, controls, and integration.

### 2. Plant productivity
The future-state flow is designed to reduce manual traffic at the corrugator output, improve WIP control, and make the operation cleaner and easier to manage. The message is simple: more real line capacity, fewer interruptions, and a better production rhythm.

### 3. Safety and traffic reduction
Ingetrans and the conveyor layer are not cosmetic additions. They are the mechanism that reduces forklift traffic, decreases uncontrolled circulation, and lowers exposure to reel handling and internal logistics risks.

### 4. Scalability
The proposed architecture is AMR-ready and automation-ready. That matters because it prevents PSC from locking itself into a fixed, hard-to-evolve layout.

## Financial Reading for Capex
The project is structured as a phased engineering and execution program. That is positive for cash control because it avoids a single large lump-sum purchase and ties payments to visible milestones.

The current documentation already supports a phased model with engineering, disassembly, transport, upgrade, installation, commissioning, and training steps. That structure should be kept.

## Main Risks to Close Before Release
1. Site survey is missing or incomplete.
2. Final utility capacity for electrical, steam, and civil interfaces must be confirmed.
3. Written BHS support for disassembly, service, and after-sales should be formalized.
4. The capex number must be reconciled across the PDF, Excel model, and user target.
5. Baseline OEE and production data are not yet visible enough for a hard ROI claim.

## Recommended Approval Path
### Gate 1: Technical validation
Approve the site survey, utility check, and final layout confirmation.

### Gate 2: Commercial validation
Freeze the final scope, reconcile the investment amount, and close the payment schedule.

### Gate 3: Execution readiness
Issue the go-ahead only after BHS support, logistics, and commissioning ownership are written down.

## Bottom Line
PSC Visalia should treat this as a strategic industrial transformation project. The value case is strong because it combines capacity recovery, risk reduction, and layout modernization in one move. The only thing blocking a clean investment story is the need to reconcile the final capex basis and validate the site readiness.
